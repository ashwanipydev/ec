Modal Verbs

The words that make you sound professional, polite, and confident in English — especially in the office.

What is a modal verb?

Modal verbs are helper verbs that come before the main verb. They show possibility, permission, ability, obligation, or politeness.

Hindi में: ये वो words हैं जो बताते हैं — "सकता हूँ, चाहिए, होगा, होना चाहिए"

Formula: Subject + Modal + Main Verb (base form)

Example: I can speak. / She should send the email. / We must finish.

The golden rule

After a modal verb, the main verb is always in base form — no 's', no 'ing', no 'ed'.

❌ Wrong
She can goes.
You should sending.
I must finished.
✅ Right
She can go.
You should send.
I must finish.

Quick memory tips

More formal →
can → could → would
Each step = more polite
Never change
No cans, wills, shoulds — modal verbs never take 's' or 'ed'
Should vs Must
Should = advice (gentle)
Must = necessary (strong)
May vs Might
May = 50% chance
Might = 20-30% chance
