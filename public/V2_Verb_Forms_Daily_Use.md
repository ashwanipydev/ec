# Important V2 Verb Forms for Daily Use
### Complete Reference Guide for Hindi-Speaking English Learners

---

> **V1** = Base form (present) | **V2** = Past tense | **V3** = Past participle
> Use **V2** when talking about something that happened in the past.
> Example: "I **went** to office." / "She **sent** the email." / "We **had** a meeting."

---

## The 4 Groups — Learn Patterns, Not Random Lists

---

### Group 1: Same in All Forms — V1 = V2 = V3
> याद करना सबसे आसान! Same word, no change at all.

| V1 (base) | V2 (past) | V3 (participle) | Hindi | Example Sentence |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|-------|-----------------| 
| put | put | put | रखना / डालना | I put the files in the folder. |
| cut | cut | cut | काटना | We cut the budget. |
| set | set | set | सेट करना | I set a reminder. |
| hit | hit | hit | मारना / हासिल करना | We hit the target. |
| let | let | let | होने देना | She let me present first. |
| cost | cost | cost | लागत होना | The project cost more than planned. |
| read | read | read | पढ़ना | I read your proposal. |

## Special Verbs — Be (am/is/are)

| V1 | V2 | V3 | Hindi | Example |
|----|----|----|-------|---------|
| am/is | was | been | था / थी | I was in a meeting. |
| are | were | been | थे / थीं | We were on a call. |

> **Rule:** Singular (I/he/she/it) → **was** | Plural (we/they/you) → **were**

## Quick Memory Tricks

**For "thought / bought / brought / fought / taught / caught":**
All end in **-ought** or **-aught**. Learn one, learn them all.
- think → **thought**
- buy → **bought**
- bring → **brought**
- teach → **taught**
- catch → **caught**

**For "sent / spent / lent / bent / meant / rent":**
All end in **-ent**. Vowel + n + t pattern.
- send → **sent**
- spend → **spent**
- lend → **lent**
- mean → **meant**

**For "slept / kept / wept / swept":**
All end in **-ept**.
- sleep → **slept**
- keep → **kept**

## 15 Daily Practice Sentences

Use these every morning to practice V2 forms naturally:

1. "I **went** to the office and **had** three meetings."
2. "She **sent** the report and **got** approval."
3. "He **spoke** to the client and **took** notes."
4. "I **wrote** an email and **forgot** to attach the file."
5. "We **met** the team and **discussed** the plan."
6. "I **thought** about your suggestion and **found** it helpful."
7. "She **left** early because she **felt** unwell."
8. "We **built** the feature and **completed** testing."
9. "I **woke** up at 7, **ate** breakfast, and **drove** to work."
10. "He **told** me the deadline and I **understood** immediately."
11. "She **chose** the best option and **led** the discussion."
12. "I **read** your proposal and **replied** with feedback."
13. "We **began** the project on Monday and **worked** non-stop."
14. "He **brought** up a good point and **explained** it clearly."
15. "I **spent** 2 hours on the task and **finished** it on time."
